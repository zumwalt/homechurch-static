70% of Nashvillians
are homeless.

by Jason McAnally
November 10, 2015

Maybe not physically. Many of us have roofs over our heads, but we're homeless, nonetheless — and in the worst sort of way. We now have the capability to connect instantly with people across the world, yet we feel more disconnected than ever. We can travel anywhere, but we don't feel like we belong anywhere. We have access to encyclopedias of knowledge in our pockets but don't know the things that matter most. And we can reveal the most intimate aspects of our hearts and lives and bodies to strangers and still feel completely unknown.

We know it. We feel it, too.

Home Church begins with the belief that God enters into both beauty and brokenness and builds a home there. That God doesn't live in cathedrals but in human hearts. We believe God is making a home in Nashville and is wanting to cure spiritual homelessness--that longing in all of us for meaning, that longing to belong, that longing to see and be truly seen, to find out why we are here and find out how we can change the world.

Our Goal

The goal of Home Church is to bring an end to spiritual homelessness in Nashville.

We're tired of pretending like everything is ok and that we're ok. We want to be a community of people where it's ok to be yourself, to not have everything already figured out, to be honest about what you believe and what you don't, to be vulnerable about where you've been and where you are. And we want to be a community of people who encourage experience of God in such a way that someday we will be ok. We want to be a community who believes that things can be better and actively works to make them better, who believes that God isn't just concerned with getting us to heaven but with getting heaven to us and in us and our friends and families and strangers and enemies.

We know — it's a ridiculous goal, and we are probably a little naive. But we're ok with that. A famous journalist once said, "Pessimists are usually right, and optimists are usually wrong but all the great changes have been accomplished by optimists."
